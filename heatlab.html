E3 · Explain

The Science of Heat

Now the formal names for what you discovered. Keep this open while solving problems.

Temperature
T (°C or K)
Average kinetic energy per particle. Measured with a thermometer. Does NOT depend on amount of substance.
Heat (Thermal Energy)
Q = mcΔT
Q = heat (J) · m = mass (kg) · c = specific heat · ΔT = temp change
Conduction
contact →
Heat transfers through direct particle contact. Needs a solid medium. Best in metals.
Convection
↑ hot ↓ cold
Heat transfers through fluid movement. Hot fluid rises, cold sinks. Needs liquid or gas.
Radiation
∿∿∿ →
Heat transfers as electromagnetic waves. Needs NO medium. Works in vacuum (space!).
The Key Difference
T ≠ Q
High temperature ≠ high heat. A spark is hotter than a pool but has far less total thermal energy.
💡 Answer to the opening question:

The swimming pool has more heat! Even though the coffee is hotter (higher temperature), the pool contains billions more water molecules. Total thermal energy (heat) = temperature × mass × specific heat. The pool wins because of its enormous mass.

E3 · Transfer Methods

3 Ways Heat Travels

Every transfer of heat in the universe happens through one of these three methods. Click each to see the live simulation.

Conduction

Heat travels through direct contact between particles. Faster-moving (hot) particles collide with slower (cold) neighbours and pass on kinetic energy — like a line of dominoes.

Needs medium
Must have solid/liquid contact
Best in metals
Free electrons carry energy fast
Real examples
🥄
Metal spoon in hot soup
Handle gets hot through direct contact
🍳
Frying pan on stove
Heat conducts from burner through metal
🧊
Ice cube in your hand
Heat conducts from hand to ice — hand feels cold
Ahmad's Note: "Conductors pass energy on freely — like a good teacher passing knowledge through direct connection."
Convection

Heat travels through the movement of fluid (liquid or gas). Hot fluid expands, becomes less dense, and rises. Cool fluid sinks to take its place — creating a circulation loop called a convection current.

Needs fluid
Liquid or gas only
Makes currents
Hot rises · cold falls
Real examples
🌊
Boiling water
Hot water rises from base, cool water sinks down
🏠
Room radiator
Heats air above it, which rises and circulates the room
🌍
Ocean currents & wind
Massive convection currents drive weather and climate
Ahmad's Note: "Convection never stops moving — like resilient communities that keep circulating and supporting each other."
Radiation

Heat travels as electromagnetic waves (infrared radiation). Unlike conduction and convection, radiation requires absolutely no medium — it travels perfectly through the vacuum of space.

No medium
Works in vacuum/space
Speed of light
3 × 10⁸ m/s
Real examples
☀
Sunlight warming Earth
150 million km of empty space — no problem for radiation
🔥
Feeling a bonfire's warmth
You feel heat without touching the fire
🌡
Thermal camera imaging
Detects infrared radiation from warm bodies
Ahmad's Note: "Radiation asks nothing of the space between — its message reaches you across the void, like hope across distance."
